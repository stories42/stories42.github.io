Gallipoli

2020-10-15

We parked at Corsa Roma half of mile away from La Fontane Greca. It was an exceptionally hot day. Everything around was closed. One or one and a half hours till the end of siesta. We decided to take a look at the sea. Regretted it immediately. The heat was unbearable, no surprises that the Italians prefer to sit this time out. To cool it down we got the ice and beverages at Bar Fontana Greca. We decided to proceed with our quest and see the old town of Gallipoli.

We took a path along the harbour. The men were waiting in hangars for a fish.

Some boots are attracted by local buyers. The plastic boxes full of the sea fish were sold very quickly to, as I got, to fish restaurant owners.

The old stones of the houses maybe were an attraction for the tourist lot, but in fact, these houses are still being used for a normal living.

An old woman opened the shutters and looked outside. I bowed embarrassingly and moved away.

At the next corner, we saw a family of six or seven people was sitting on the bench. In the middle, the mother washed the face of her disabled teenage boy from a plastic basin. Then she moved him gently to the corner of the bench and washed his feet. This scene touched me deeply but I decided not to pull the camera out. But that year the crowd made us worried. We put the masks on and backed into our car. Do we still be able to enjoy the beauty of life?

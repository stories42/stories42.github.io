Alessano

2020-08-09

We sat on the bench just outside Chiesa Madre del Santissimo Salvatore. They put the plastic chairs in front of the church. The service is scheduled to be outdoors. Probably for the next years to come. The orchestra was practicing while the crowd gathered. The nun has read something, the people occasionally repeated “Amen” or sang “Hallelujah”. Then the padre took a speech. As we know not a word in Italian we didn’t get a clue what the padre has spoken about. His voices went up and down. We noticed how his resonant voice collided with the face of a very old man. We recognized the words coronavirus, rispetto, famiglia. I thought about uncertainty. About how it is the scariest thing in life.

“It would take some time. Maybe we just order a pizza?”, she asked. “What do you want?”

“I don’t care much,” I answered abruptly. “Maybe we get a beer and go to the apartment. We can watch fireworks from there.”
